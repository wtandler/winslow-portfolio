## The Problem

Legacy intake is a bottleneck by design. A request comes in and waits in a queue until a reviewer can work through it—reading, researching, gathering context, assessing—before anyone decides anything. That review labor is slow, expensive, and gated on a small number of expensive people. The reviewer spends most of their time on research, not judgment, and throughput is capped by how fast humans can read.

## The Reframe

That whole model assumes analysis and research are scarce, expensive labor. They aren't anymore. When AI can do the reading, research, and preparation live—at a marginal cost near zero—intake itself can be reimagined.

So flip the division of labor: **AI analyzes and prepares; humans judge and decide.** The reviewer stops spending their day on research and spends it on the only thing that actually needs a person—the call.

## What the Prototype Does

A working front-end for that flipped model:

* **Submit** — a request comes in through a simple form.
* **Analyze** — the agent researches and prepares each request live, attaching a **confidence** and the assembled context.
* **Queue** — everything lands triaged, so the obvious cases are obvious and the hard ones are flagged.
* **Review** — a human opens only what needs judgment, with the research already done.
* **Analysis** — a view over what's flowing through the system.

The point isn't to automate the decision. It's to delete the research labor that used to sit in front of it.

## Status

A functional prototype proving out the loop and the AI-confidence UX—not yet a shipped product. The bet it's testing: once research labor is effectively free, how much of intake collapses into "AI prepares, human decides," and how do you make that handoff legible enough that a reviewer actually trusts it?
