## The Problem

Legacy intake is a bottleneck by design. A request comes in and waits in a queue until a reviewer can work through it: reading, researching, gathering context, assessing. Only then does anyone decide anything. That review labor is slow, and it's gated on a small number of expensive people. The reviewer spends most of their time on research rather than judgment, and throughput is capped by how fast humans can read.

## The Reframe

That whole model assumes analysis and research are scarce, expensive labor. They aren't anymore. When AI can do the reading and preparation live, at very low cost, the process can be reorganized.

The division of labor flips: AI analyzes and prepares, humans judge and decide. The reviewer stops spending their day on research and spends it on the part that actually needs a person, the decision.

## What the Prototype Does

A standalone system built around that flipped model: chat in front, an agent in the middle, a human at the end.

* Submit: a request comes in through a chat interface instead of a form.
* Research: the agent traverses Microsoft Graph to assemble the context a reviewer used to chase down by hand, then attaches its findings and a confidence score to each request.
* Queue: requests land already triaged, with the hard cases flagged for attention.
* Review: a human opens only what needs judgment, with the research already done.
* Analysis: a view over what's flowing through the system.

The decision still belongs to a human. The system removes the research work that used to sit in front of it.

## Status

A functional prototype that proves out the loop and the confidence UX, not yet a shipped product. The questions it's testing: how much of intake can move to "AI prepares, human decides," and can the handoff be made clear enough that a reviewer actually trusts it?
